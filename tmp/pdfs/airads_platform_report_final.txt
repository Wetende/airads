Executive Summary
       Airads has already moved beyond the original LMS assignment. The current platform combines the public
       college website, campuses, admissions, Virtual Campus, online course delivery, assessments, certificates,
       payments, learner operations and external learning integrations in one system.

       The self-paced online-learning foundation is working. The platform can create and publish courses, enrol
       learners, deliver video, text, document, quiz and assignment activities, track progress, calculate grades and issue
       certificates. Focused verification completed for this report found no Django system-check issues and all 158
       selected tests passed.

       The remaining work is institution-wide and integration-heavy. A complete Airads platform now requires a
       separate Campus Operations workspace, ERP connectivity, student and staff portals, mature live classes,
       production Google services, SMS, secure content administration, management reporting, monitoring, staff
       training and controlled rollout.

       Approval is requested for a four-month continuation at twice the previous contract rate. The role is no
       longer limited to LMS feature development. It now includes solution architecture, sensitive institutional-data
       integration, third-party vendor coordination, security, testing, deployment, training and post-launch stabilisation
       across the whole college platform.

The assignment has changed from an LMS into a college-wide digital system
The original LMS problem was primarily about learning content, assessments and learner progress. The current
requirement covers the full journey from public discovery and admission through campus administration, fee visibility,
student and staff services, online and live teaching, communication, reporting and executive oversight.

This change matters because the platform now sits between multiple systems and departments. A wrong student
identifier can affect enrolment and fees. A failed notification can affect admission or class attendance. A weak
permission boundary can expose academic, financial or staff information. A failed ERP reconciliation can create
conflicting institutional records. These are operational responsibilities, not ordinary website enhancements.

Airads therefore needs two connected workspaces with one identity and permission model:

    1. Campus Operations workspace: admissions, student records, campuses, finance visibility, staff services,
       communications, reports and ERP-linked workflows.
    2. LMS and Virtual Campus workspace: courses, curriculum, instructors, learning progress, assessments, live
       sessions, Google Classroom, grades and certificates.

The workspaces must remain connected so a learner, programme, campus, payment and academic record can be
traced consistently without forcing every employee into the LMS interface.

The four-month continuation must complete the institutional platform
The continuation should be managed as one integrated completion programme. Each deliverable below is required
because omitting it would leave a critical part of the college operating outside the platform or create duplicated,
unreliable records.

    1. Campus Operations workspace and access model. Admissions, finance, campus administrators and
       management perform different work from learners and instructors.
    2. ERP selection, data mapping and integration. Student IDs, admissions, programmes, enrolments, fees and
       campuses cannot remain duplicated across independent systems.
    3. Student and staff service portals. A complete college platform must support institutional services beyond online
       course playback.
    4. Production-ready live classes. Live and blended programmes require reliable scheduling, attendance
       evidence, recordings and reminders.
    5. Google Cloud, Workspace, Classroom and sign-in completion. Integration code cannot operate safely
       without institution-owned accounts, permissions and administration policy.
    6. Production email and SMS communications. Admissions, payments, classes and reminders require traceable
       channels with consent and failure recovery.
    7. Payment and finance reconciliation. A successful online payment must create the correct admission, fee and
       course-access state.
    8. Secure website content administration. News, events, campuses and forms must be maintained by authorised
       staff rather than hardcoded or browser-local processes.
    9. Unified reporting, audit and integration monitoring. Management needs one reliable view, while technical
       staff must detect failed synchronisation before operations are affected.
   10. Security, testing, training and phased rollout. Institutional data and daily workflows cannot be moved safely
       into production through code delivery alone.

External integrations are business systems, not simple sign-ins
ERP integration must map student identifiers, campuses, programmes, enrolments, fee status and ownership of each
field. It must include reconciliation, duplicate handling, retry logic, audit records and a support procedure for
mismatched data. The ERP should remain authoritative for the institutional records agreed by management, while the
LMS remains authoritative for learning activity, progress, submissions and grades.

Google Classroom should remain optional at course level. The code already supports OAuth connection, course
linking, roster comparison, invitations, publication of LMS lessons and assessments as Classroom links,
announcements, durable sync jobs and grade passback. Production completion still requires an Airads-controlled
Google Cloud project, approved scopes, secure credentials, Workspace policy, scheduled job execution and a
controlled pilot.

Google Meet, Zoom, Teams and streaming providers serve live sessions, not self-paced lessons. The platform now
has scheduled-session records, provider-aware presentation, passcodes, recordings, calendar links and manual
attendance. It still needs provider lifecycle automation where selected, verified attendance import, reminders, focused
tests and operational support rules. The LMS must continue to own course progress and final academic records.

Email and SMS must become governed communication channels. Email and in-app delivery foundations exist,
including Brevo configuration and retryable email outbox processing. Airads still needs verified sending domains,
production credentials, message templates, consent and opt-out rules, delivery monitoring and an SMS provider
integration. SMS code is not currently present.

Payments have a strong Paystack foundation, including checkout, M-Pesa charge flows, webhooks, refunds and
payout records. Production readiness still requires live merchant onboarding, webhook verification, finance procedures
and ERP reconciliation so an online payment is reflected correctly in institutional fee records.

Why completing this work is important now
       One reliable student journey: applicants should not be re-entered manually into separate website, ERP, finance
       and LMS systems.
       Operational control: admissions, finance, instructors and campus administrators need role-specific information
       without relying on spreadsheets and informal messages.
       Accurate fees and enrolment: payment, admission and course access must reconcile before the system is
       trusted for daily operations.
       Live and flexible learning: self-paced learning is established, but Airads also needs governed real-time classes,
       attendance, recordings and reminders for live and blended programmes.
       Institutional communication: email and SMS delivery must be traceable, consent-aware and recoverable when
       a provider fails.
       Management visibility: executives need unified summaries across campuses, admissions, finance and learning
       rather than disconnected reports.
       Security and continuity: moving institutional operations online increases responsibility for permissions, audit
       logs, backups, incident response and staff training.
       Protection of the existing investment: continued ownership during integration and rollout reduces the risk of
       fragmented vendor work, undocumented decisions and regressions in the functioning LMS.


Commercial recommendation: renew for four months at twice the previous rate
The proposed rate adjustment is based on the change in responsibility, risk and expected output. The continuation
covers more than feature coding:

       institution-wide solution architecture and data ownership design;
       ERP, Google, communications and payment-provider integration;
       student, staff, campus, finance and learning access controls;
       migration, reconciliation, failure recovery and auditability;
       production security, monitoring, backup and support readiness;
       cross-department requirements, vendor coordination and acceptance testing;
       staff training, controlled rollout and post-launch stabilisation; and
       protection and maintenance of the working website, admissions and LMS capabilities during major integration
       work.

A two-times contract rate is therefore recommended as a scope-adjusted continuation fee, not merely a time extension
of the original LMS contract. Third-party subscriptions, SMS charges, Google Workspace licensing, ERP vendor fees,
payment-provider charges and infrastructure costs should be budgeted separately by Airads.

Requested approval: continue the current technical ownership for four months under a revised contract at twice the
previous rate, with the deliverables in this report forming the basis of the statement of work and acceptance review.
Completion should be measured by operational acceptance
The continuation should be considered successful when:

    1. authorised users can enter the correct Campus Operations or LMS workspace using one governed identity;
    2. agreed student, staff, programme, campus, enrolment and fee data synchronises with the ERP and exceptions
       are visible;
    3. self-paced, live-online, streamed and blended learning journeys operate with reliable progress and attendance
       evidence;
    4. Google Classroom and selected live-class providers pass a controlled production pilot;
    5. admissions, payment, class and reminder notifications are delivered through approved email and SMS providers
       with logs and retries;
    6. management reporting reconciles website, ERP, payment and LMS information;
    7. sensitive administration is server-controlled, role-based and auditable;
    8. backup, monitoring, incident handling and recovery procedures are documented and tested; and
    9. staff and pilot users complete acceptance testing before phased rollout across campuses.


Decisions required from the CEO and department heads
Technical implementation can proceed efficiently only after the institution confirms the following operating decisions.

    1. Select the ERP and confirm its APIs, vendor support and obligations. Integration design depends on the actual
       ERP contract and interfaces.
    2. Define system ownership for student, programme, enrolment, fee, staff and learning records. Ownership rules
       prevent conflicting records and unsafe overwrites.
    3. Approve first-release departments, roles and permissions. The operations workspace must reflect real
       accountability.
    4. Confirm Google Workspace administration and live-class providers. Google sign-in, Classroom, Meet and
       account governance require institution-owned policy.
    5. Approve email, SMS, payment and infrastructure providers and budgets. Provider accounts, transaction
       charges and sender approvals are institutional costs.
    6. Nominate departmental representatives and a pilot campus or programme. Data mapping and acceptance
       require authorised process owners.
    7. Approve the four-month continuation and revised contract rate. Continuous ownership is needed across
       integration, testing, rollout and stabilisation.

Caveats and Assumptions
This assessment is based on the current platform codebase and focused automated verification, not a live production
audit of every campus or external vendor account. Django reported no system-check issues and 158 selected tests
passed. The review did not confirm production ERP access, Google Workspace administration, live email or SMS
contracts, merchant settlement, production backups or end-user acceptance.

External provider capabilities and exact implementation details will depend on the ERP selected, API access granted by
each vendor, Airads data-governance decisions and the availability of departmental representatives for mapping and
acceptance. The live-session module has a meaningful software foundation but requires focused automated tests and
production-provider validation before it should be considered institutionally complete.
